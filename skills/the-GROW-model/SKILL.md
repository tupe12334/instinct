# The GROW Model

## Overview

A 4-stage framework for structured coaching conversations, problem-solving sessions, and goal achievement. Originally developed by Sir John Whitmore.

```
G — Goal      What do you want to achieve?
R — Reality   Where are you now?
O — Options   What could you do?
W — Will      What will you do?
```

GROW moves from aspiration → current state → possibilities → commitment. Each stage builds on the last.

## Stages

### G — Goal

Define a clear, motivating target. The goal should be specific enough to know when it's reached.

Key questions:
- What do you want to achieve?
- What would success look like?
- By when?
- Why is this important to you?
- How will you know you've reached it?
- What would achieving this give you?

Tips:
- Distinguish between **end goals** (ultimate outcome) and **performance goals** (what you directly control)
- Make it challenging but believable
- Apply smart/pure/clear criteria to sharpen the goal

### R — Reality

Assess the current situation honestly, without judgment. This stage grounds the conversation in facts.

Key questions:
- What is happening now?
- What have you already tried?
- What's working? What's not?
- What resources do you have?
- What's blocking you?
- On a scale of 1–10, where are you relative to your goal?
- What do others say about this situation?

Tips:
- Stay descriptive, not evaluative — "what is" not "what should be"
- Surface assumptions that may be constraining thinking
- Don't rush to solutions here; understanding the reality fully is the work

### O — Options

Generate the widest possible set of potential actions before narrowing. This stage is divergent thinking.

Key questions:
- What could you do?
- What else? (ask this 3–5 times)
- What would you do if [constraint] didn't exist?
- What would someone you admire do?
- What have others done in similar situations?
- What's the smallest possible step?
- What's the boldest possible move?

Tips:
- Don't evaluate options during generation — capture everything
- Aim for at least 5–7 options; more is better
- Include "do nothing" as an option to make its costs explicit
- After generation, evaluate each option on feasibility and impact

### W — Will (Way Forward)

Convert the best options into concrete commitments with accountability.

Key questions:
- What will you do?
- When exactly will you do it?
- What could get in the way? How will you handle that?
- On a scale of 1–10, how committed are you? (If <8, what would raise it?)
- Who needs to know about this?
- What support do you need?
- How and when will you review progress?

Tips:
- Specificity = accountability. "I'll do X by [date]" beats "I'll try to"
- Low commitment score (1–7) signals the chosen option is wrong — revisit Options
- Build in a review checkpoint

## How to Run a GROW Session

1. **Set context** (2 min): explain the 4 stages, confirm the person has ownership
2. **Goal** (5–10 min): agree on what you're working toward in this session
3. **Reality** (10–15 min): explore the current state fully before jumping ahead
4. **Options** (10–15 min): generate options without judging, then evaluate
5. **Will** (5–10 min): commit to specific actions with dates and accountability

Total: 30–60 minutes. Can be compressed to 10 min for quick check-ins.

## Output Format

```
GROW SESSION: [topic/person/date]

GOAL
Target: [what they want to achieve]
Success looks like: [measurable outcome]
Timeline: [by when]
Why it matters: [motivation]

REALITY
Current state: [where they are now, rating X/10]
Already tried: [previous attempts]
Key blockers: [what's in the way]
Resources available: [what they have]

OPTIONS GENERATED
1. [option] — feasibility: [H/M/L], impact: [H/M/L]
2. [option] — feasibility: [H/M/L], impact: [H/M/L]
3. [option] — ...
[selected]: [chosen option and why]

WILL — COMMITMENTS
Action 1: [what] by [when]
Action 2: [what] by [when]
Obstacles anticipated: [risk] → [mitigation]
Commitment score: [X/10]
Review checkpoint: [date/method]
```

## Common Mistakes

- Jumping from G straight to W (skipping Reality and Options)
- Offering advice during Options instead of asking questions
- Accepting vague commitments in Will ("I'll look into it")
- Low commitment score without revisiting the option or goal
